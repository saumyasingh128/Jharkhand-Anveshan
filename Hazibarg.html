About Hazaribagh

Deriving its name from "Hazar" (Thousand) and "Bagh" (Gardens), this scenic city is located on the Chota Nagpur Plateau. It is famous for its wildlife sanctuary, scenic hills, and pleasant weather year-round.

A Health Resort & Edu-Hub

Hazaribagh is often called a health resort due to its pristine air and dense forests. The iconic Hazaribagh Lake is the heart of the city, surrounded by colonial-era bungalows and vibrant student life.

It is home to the famous Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary, which hosts sambars, nilgais, and sloth bears. For the adventurous, a trek up to Canary Hill offers a panoramic view of the entire city.

The city is also a major educational hub of Jharkhand, housing the prestigious Vinoba Bhave University and the historic St. Columba's College.

Taste of Hazaribagh

From crispy lakeside snacks to traditional sweets, Hazaribagh offers a simple yet delicious culinary experience.

Singara (Samosa)

The Samosas (locally called Singara) near Hazaribagh Lake are legendary. Served with spicy chutney, they are the favorite evening snack for students and locals alike.

5.0

Balushahi

A famous sweet of the region. Hazaribagh's Balushahi is known for its flaky texture and perfect sweetness, often made with pure desi ghee.

4.5

Duska-Barra

A staple breakfast. The Duska here is often paired with 'Barra' (vada) and a spicy potato-chickpea curry, available at street corners in the morning.

5.0

Malpua

During festivals like Holi, Hazaribagh households prepare rich, syrup-soaked Malpuas. Some local sweet shops serve this delicacy year-round.

4.0

Culture & Heritage

Hazaribagh is a unique blend of tribal art, colonial history, and vibrant religious festivals.

Life in Hazaribagh

International Ram Navami

Hazaribagh is world-famous for its Ram Navami processions. Hundreds of 'Akharas' display traditional martial arts with swords and sticks, attracting lakhs of devotees.

Sohrai & Khovar Art

The walls of mud houses in Hazaribagh villages are often painted with GI-tagged Sohrai and Khovar art, a centuries-old matriarchal tradition depicting nature and wildlife.

The Student Vibe

With VBU and St. Columba's, the city has a youthful energy. The cafes near Jhanda Chowk and the Lake area are always buzzing with students debating politics and exams.

Morning Walk Culture

The culture of morning walks at Hazaribagh Lake is almost a ritual. The fresh air and serene water attract people of all ages before the city wakes up.
