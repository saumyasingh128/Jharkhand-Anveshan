About Giridih

Deriving its name from "Giri" (Hills) and "Dih" (Land), Giridih is a city of hills, forests, and waterfalls. It holds immense spiritual significance as the site of Parasnath (Sammed Shikharji), the most important Jain pilgrimage.

Nature & Spirituality

Giridih is globally famous for Shikharji (Parasnath), located on the highest peak of Jharkhand. It is believed that 20 out of 24 Jain Tirthankaras attained salvation here.

Apart from spirituality, the city is blessed with natural beauty. The Usri Falls is a mesmerizing picnic spot where the river Usri falls in three gorges. The Khandoli Dam offers adventure sports like boating and paragliding.

Historically known as the "Land of Mica", Giridih was once a major mining hub, contributing to its rich industrial heritage along with its dense forests.

Local Delicacies

Giridih offers a mix of Jharkhandi tribal food and Bihari sweets, with a special emphasis on pure vegetarian food due to Jain influence.

Tilkut

Though famous in Gaya, Giridih also produces excellent Tilkut (sesame sweet), especially during winters (Makar Sankranti). Crunchy and sweet!

Culture of Giridih

Giridih is a melting pot where the peace of spirituality meets the thrill of adventure and the rawness of mining.

Life in the Hills

Spiritual Hub

The town of Madhuban at the foot of Parasnath is always bustling with Jain pilgrims in white robes, creating an atmosphere of peace and devotion distinct to Giridih.

Adventure & Khandoli

Khandoli Park is the city's playground. Locals flock here for boating, bird watching, and adventure sports like parasailing and rock climbing.

Land of Mica & Coal

The economy is driven by mining. You can often see the hustle of trade related to mica and coal, giving the city an industrial undertone beneath its green cover.

Tribal Heritage

The region has a significant Santhal population. During festivals like Karma and Sohrai, the beats of the mandar (drum) resonate through the villages.
