Wild & Protected

Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary, established in 1975, is home to rich biodiversity including elephants, leopards, barking deer, and numerous bird species. The sanctuary serves as an important elephant migration corridor.

The Gentle Giants

Dalma is a major corridor for Indian Elephants. Spot them near watering holes during early mornings and late evenings.

Flora

The sanctuary boasts Sal, Mahua, Bamboo, and numerous medicinal plants in its mixed deciduous forests.

Fauna

Home to elephants, leopards, barking deer, sloth bears, porcupines, and a rich variety of reptiles and birds.

Birdwatching

Over 150 species including peafowl, red jungle fowl, crested serpent eagle, and various migratory birds.

Spiritual Journey

Ancient Shiva Temple at the Peak

At the highest point of Dalma Hills stands an ancient Shiva temple, believed to be centuries old. Pilgrims and trekkers alike make the journey to seek blessings and experience the profound peace at this elevation.

Spiritual Significance

The temple is especially crowded during Maha Shivratri and Shravan month.

Trek to the Temple

A 3km trek from the base leads to the temple, offering stunning views along the way.
