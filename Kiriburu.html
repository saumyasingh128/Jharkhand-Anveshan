Red Earth, Green Soul

Kiriburu and Meghahatuburu, the twin mining towns, are famous for their breathtaking sunset points, massive open-cast iron ore mines, and the sprawling Saranda forest that surrounds them.

Visitor Information

Everything you need to know before visiting the Land of 700 Hills

Best Time to Visit

Monsoon (July - September)

Lush green forests, mist-covered hills, and flowing waterfalls. The landscape is at its most vibrant.

Winter (October - February)

Clear skies, pleasant weather (5°C to 20°C), and perfect for sunset viewing and photography.

Avoid Summer (March - June)

Hot and humid, with temperatures reaching up to 40°C. Limited visibility due to haze.

How to Reach

By Road

From Ranchi: 160 km via NH143 (approx 4-5 hours). Well-maintained roads through forest areas.

Nearest Railway

Chaibasa (65 km) or Rourkela (90 km). From there, taxis are available to Kiriburu.

Nearest Airport

Birsa Munda Airport, Ranchi (160 km). Direct flights from major Indian cities.

Important Notes

Permits

No permit required for general tourism. Mining areas have restricted access.

Accommodation

SAIL Guest House available. Limited private hotels in Kiriburu/Meghahatuburu.

Food & Facilities

Basic eateries available. Carry water and snacks for long journeys.
