About Lodh Falls (Buria Pahad)

Lodh Falls, also known as Buria Pahad Falls, is the highest waterfall in Jharkhand and one of the tallest in India. Located in the Latehar district, this magnificent waterfall cascades from a staggering height of 143 meters (469 feet).

The waterfall is formed by the Burha River as it plunges down the steep cliffs of the Netarhat plateau. The name "Buria Pahad" comes from the local tribal language, with "Buria" meaning "old" and "Pahad" meaning "mountain," referring to the ancient geological formations.

What makes Lodh Falls particularly unique is its multi-tiered structure, with water cascading in multiple stages down the rocky face. The falls are surrounded by dense Sal forests and are part of a rich biodiversity hotspot.

Extreme Height Danger Warning

Swimming is strictly prohibited at Lodh Falls. The extreme height creates powerful impact forces, and the pool at the base has dangerous undercurrents.

The 143-meter drop creates tremendous force at the base. Rocks are extremely slippery, and the mist makes surfaces treacherous. Several fatal accidents have occurred. Viewing platforms are provided for safe observation - always stay within marked areas and never attempt to climb the rocks or enter the water.
