Chhinnamasta - The Headless Goddess

Rajrappa Temple is one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, where it is believed that the head (Mastak) of Goddess Sati fell when Lord Shiva carried her burning body. The presiding deity is worshipped as Chhinnamasta - the self-decapitated goddess.

The temple is situated at the confluence of the Bhairavi and Damodar rivers, creating a powerful spiritual energy center. The main idol depicts Goddess Chhinnamasta standing on the bodies of Kamdev and Rati, holding her own severed head in one hand and a sword in the other, with three streams of blood flowing from her neck.

Spiritual Significance

Understanding the deep symbolism and power of Goddess Chhinnamasta

Self-Sacrifice

Chhinnamasta represents the ultimate sacrifice - cutting off one's own head to feed others, symbolizing transcendence of ego and attachment.

Life-Death Cycle

The three streams of blood represent the three gunas (qualities) and the continuous cycle of creation, preservation, and destruction.

Tantric Power

As a major Tantric center, the temple represents the awakening of Kundalini energy and spiritual transformation.

Historical Significance

Discover the ancient legends and historical importance of Rajrappa Temple

Legend & Origin

According to Hindu mythology, after Goddess Sati immolated herself in Daksha's yagna, Lord Shiva carried her burning body across the universe. To calm Shiva's destructive Tandava dance, Lord Vishnu used his Sudarshana Chakra to cut Sati's body into 51 pieces, which fell at different places on Earth.

Sati's head (Mastak) fell at Rajrappa, making it one of the most important Shakti Peethas. The temple has been a major pilgrimage site for centuries, attracting both mainstream Hindu devotees and Tantric practitioners.

The current temple structure dates back several centuries and has been renovated multiple times. It remains one of the most powerful and mysterious spiritual sites in Eastern India.

Special Tantric Offerings

At Rajrappa Temple, special offerings are made to Goddess Chhinnamasta that include red hibiscus flowers, vermilion, red cloth, and specific fruits. During Tantric rituals, offerings may also include specific grains, pulses, and symbolic items representing the five elements.

The temple is unique in that it accepts both vegetarian and non-vegetarian offerings (in specific Tantric rituals), reflecting the goddess's fierce nature. However, regular daily worship includes only vegetarian offerings that are then distributed as prasad.

A Unique Blend of Devotion & Mysticism

The atmosphere at Rajrappa Temple is unlike any other. There's a palpable sense of raw, primal energy combined with deep devotion. The sound of the converging rivers, the chanting of mantras, and the occasional Tantric rituals create a powerful sensory experience.

You'll encounter a diverse mix of people - mainstream Hindu pilgrims offering coconuts and flowers, Tantric practitioners performing esoteric rituals, curious tourists, and local tribal communities who have worshipped here for generations. Despite the diversity, there's a shared sense of reverence for the powerful goddess.

Major Festivals & Rituals

Navratri & Durga Puja

The most important festival with 9 days of special pujas, Tantric rituals, and grand celebrations. Thousands flock to the temple during this time.

Amavasya (New Moon)

Special Tantric rituals are performed on new moon nights. Considered especially powerful for spiritual practices and offerings to Chhinnamasta.

Kali Puja & Diwali

Special worship of the fierce aspects of the goddess. The temple is decorated with lamps and special fire rituals are performed.
