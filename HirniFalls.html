About Hirni Falls

Hirni Falls is one of the most powerful and majestic waterfalls in Jharkhand, located in the Gumla district near the Chotanagpur plateau. With a height of approximately 37 meters (121 feet), it creates a thunderous spectacle as water plummets into a deep pool below.

The falls are formed by the Hirni River, which originates in the nearby hills and creates this impressive natural wonder. The name "Hirni" is derived from the local tribal language, meaning "deer," possibly referring to the graceful yet powerful flow of the waterfall.

What makes Hirni Falls particularly remarkable is the sheer volume of water during monsoon season, when it transforms into a roaring cascade that can be heard from considerable distances. The surrounding rocky terrain and dense forests add to its wild, untamed beauty.

The Power of Nature

Understanding the sheer force behind Hirni Falls

Force & Formation

Sheer Volume

During monsoon, the waterfall carries thousands of liters of water per second, creating a thunderous roar.

Geological Formation

The waterfall was formed over millennia as the Hirni River carved through the granite rocks of the Chotanagpur Plateau.

Constant Erosion

The powerful flow continuously reshapes the rocks, creating new formations and deepening the pool below.

Critical Safety Warning

Swimming is strictly prohibited at Hirni Falls. The powerful currents, deep pool, and slippery rocks make this waterfall extremely dangerous.

The force of the waterfall creates strong undercurrents that can trap even experienced swimmers. Rocks are extremely slippery due to constant mist and moss. Several accidents have occurred due to disregard of safety rules. Always maintain a safe distance from the edge and never attempt to swim in the pool.
