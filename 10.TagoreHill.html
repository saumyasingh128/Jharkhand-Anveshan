Historical Significance

The Legacy of Tagore Hill

Formerly known as Morabadi Hill, this serene location became the ashram of Jyotirindranath Tagore, brother of Rabindranath Tagore, in 1908. Today, it stands as a symbol of literary heritage and spiritual retreat.

Historical Timeline

1908

Jyotirindranath Tagore's Ashram

The hill became the residence and ashram of Jyotirindranath Tagore, who named it "Brahma Sthal" (Place of God).

1939

Rabindranath Tagore's Visit

The Nobel laureate visited the hill, further cementing its connection to the Tagore family legacy.

1960

Development as Tourist Site

The Bihar government (now Jharkhand) developed the hill as a tourist and heritage site.

Literary & Cultural Legacy

The Tagore Connection

How Tagore Hill became intertwined with India's literary and cultural renaissance

Jyotirindranath Tagore

The elder brother of Rabindranath Tagore, Jyotirindranath was a playwright, musician, and artist who established his ashram on the hill in 1908.

Lived here 1908-1925

Rabindranath Tagore

The Nobel laureate visited the hill in 1939, drawing inspiration from its serene environment for his literary works.

Nobel Prize in Literature 1913

Cultural Renaissance

The hill became a hub for artists, writers, and thinkers during the Bengal Renaissance, influencing India's cultural development.
