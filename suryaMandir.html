The Radiant Surya Mandir

A magnificent temple dedicated to Lord Surya, the Sun God, located in Lapung, Ranchi District

Lord Surya - The Source of Light & Life

Surya Mandir in Lapung is one of the rare temples in India dedicated exclusively to Lord Surya, the Sun God. The temple is renowned for its unique architecture and the powerful spiritual energy associated with solar worship.

The main idol depicts Lord Surya standing on a chariot pulled by seven horses, representing the seven colors of sunlight and the seven days of the week. The temple is strategically oriented so that the first rays of the morning sun fall directly on the deity's feet during equinoxes.

Origin & Significance

Surya Mandir in Lapung was established in the late 20th century, inspired by the ancient Sun Temple of Konark. The temple was built to revive the ancient tradition of Surya worship which has been an integral part of Indian spirituality since Vedic times.

Sun worship has special significance in Jharkhand, particularly among tribal communities who have traditionally worshipped natural elements. The temple serves as a bridge between ancient solar worship traditions and contemporary spiritual practices.

Over the years, Surya Mandir has become an important pilgrimage site, especially during solar festivals like Makar Sankranti, Ratha Saptami, and Chhath Puja. The temple's unique architecture and spiritual significance attract visitors from across India.

Special Solar Festival Prasad

During solar festivals like Makar Sankranti and Ratha Saptami, the temple prepares special bhog that includes khichdi made with newly harvested grains, seasonal vegetables, and sweets made from jaggery and sesame. These offerings symbolize gratitude to the Sun God for agricultural abundance.

The temple also distributes special prasad during Chhath Puja consisting of thekua, fruits, and sugarcane. Devotees observe strict fasts and offer prayers to the rising and setting sun during this important festival dedicated to Lord Surya.

A Sanctuary of Light & Energy

The atmosphere at Surya Mandir is uniquely luminous and energizing. The temple seems to glow with its own light, especially during sunrise and sunset when the sun's rays interact with the temple's architecture. The air feels charged with positive solar energy.

You'll find devotees performing Surya Namaskar (sun salutations) at dawn, families offering prayers for health and prosperity, students seeking blessings for success, and spiritual seekers meditating in the temple's radiant atmosphere. The temple attracts both traditional worshippers and those interested in solar energy and architecture.

Major Solar Festivals & Rituals

Makar Sankranti

Celebrated in January when the sun enters Capricorn. Special pujas, kite flying, and distribution of sesame sweets.

Ratha Saptami

Celebrated in January-February, considered the birthday of the Sun God. Special chariot procession and rituals.

Chhath Puja

Major festival dedicated to Sun God, with offerings made to rising and setting sun. Observed with great devotion in Jharkhand.
