About Jonha Falls (Gautamdhara)

Jonha Falls, also known as Gautamdhara, is one of the most beautiful and spiritually significant waterfalls in Jharkhand. Located approximately 40 km from Ranchi, it's formed by the Raru River as it plunges 43 meters (141 feet) down a steep granite cliff.

The name "Gautamdhara" comes from the nearby Gautam Buddha statue and temple, making it an important pilgrimage site for Buddhists and spiritual seekers. The falls are particularly famous for the 722 steps that lead down to the base, offering breathtaking views at every level.

During monsoon season, the waterfall transforms into a magnificent curtain of water, while in winter it becomes a gentle, serene cascade perfect for meditation and contemplation.

Important Safety Warning

Swimming is strictly prohibited at Jonha Falls. The 722 steps can be steep and slippery, especially during monsoon season.

The pool at the base has strong currents and hidden rocks. Several accidents have occurred due to disregard of safety rules. Always use handrails, wear proper footwear, and maintain a safe distance from the edge.
