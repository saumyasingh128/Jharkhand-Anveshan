History & Significance

Pahari Mandir is located on top of a hill known as 'Ranchi Hill'. To reach the main temple of Lord Shiva, devotees must climb approximately 468 steps. From the top, one can see a breathtaking bird's eye view of the entire city of Ranchi.

Before independence, this hill was known as 'Phansi Tongri' (Hanging Hill) where freedom fighters were hanged by the British. After independence, it was transformed into a place of worship.

Uniquely, the Pahari Mandir has the distinction of hoisting the Indian National Flag on Republic Day and Independence Day, honoring the martyrs who sacrificed their lives here.

Ranchi's Delights

After the climb, enjoy the traditional flavors of Ranchi available at the foothills

Dhuska

Snack

The most famous street food of Ranchi. Deep-fried pancakes made from a batter of rice and lentils, served with spicy chickpea curry (ghugni) or potato curry.

Ranchi Special

Litti Chokha

Classic

A staple across Jharkhand. Baked wheat balls stuffed with sattu (roasted gram flour) and herbs, served with roasted eggplant and potato mash (chokha).

Must Try

Thekua

Sweet

A dry sweet snack prepared from wheat flour, jaggery, and ghee. It is crunchy, delicious, and can be stored for weeks. A popular offering during festivals.

The Hilltop Vibe

Experience serenity at 2,140 feet above sea level

Cool Breeze

As you climb higher, the city noise fades away, replaced by the cool breeze and the rustling of leaves. The top offers a serene environment perfect for meditation.

Panoramic Views

The summit provides a 360-degree view of Ranchi city. Sunrise and sunset are particularly magical times to visit, turning the sky into a canvas of colors.

Historical Echoes

The site holds a deep historical significance. The blend of patriotism and spirituality creates a unique aura that you won't find in many other temples.

Shravan Month

During the holy month of Shravan, thousands of devotees flock to Pahari Mandir to offer 'Jal' (holy water) to the Shiva Lingam. The stairway becomes a vibrant stream of saffron-clad pilgrims chanting "Har Har Mahadev".
