The Majestic Hundru Falls

Hundru Falls is one of the highest and most spectacular waterfalls in Jharkhand, formed by the Subarnarekha River as it plunges 98 meters (322 feet) down a rugged cliff face.

Located about 45 km from Ranchi, this natural wonder creates a stunning visual and auditory spectacle, especially during the monsoon season when the waterfall is at its most powerful.

The falls are surrounded by lush green forests and unique rock formations, making it a popular destination for nature lovers, trekkers, and photographers.

Geological Wonder

Understanding the unique geological formation of Hundru Falls

Formation & Features

Basalt Rock Formation

The waterfall is formed over ancient basalt rocks from the Deccan Traps, creating a dramatic vertical drop.

Subarnarekha River

The river carves through the rugged terrain, creating this spectacular waterfall along its course.

Millennia in the Making

The current form is the result of thousands of years of water erosion on the volcanic rock formations.

Critical Safety Warning

Swimming is strictly prohibited at Hundru Falls. The powerful undercurrents, unpredictable water flow, and slippery rock surfaces pose extreme danger.

Multiple accidents have occurred due to disregard of safety rules. The pool at the base has strong circular currents that can trap even experienced swimmers. Always maintain a safe distance from the edge.
