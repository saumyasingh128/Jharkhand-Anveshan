The Vision

Founded by Jamsetji Nusserwanji Tata, Jamshedpur is a marvel of urban planning. Unlike other industrial hubs, here the factory does not dominate the greenery; they co-exist.

A City of Firsts

Jamshedpur (Tatanagar) is celebrated for its wide roads, tree-lined avenues, and high quality of life. It is the only city in India without a municipal corporation, maintained entirely by JUSCO (Tata Steel Utilities).

Industrial Hub

Tata Steel, Tata Motors, and heavy engineering industries drive the economy.

Green Cover

Home to Jubilee Park (inspired by Brindavan Gardens) and Dalma Hills.

Sports

JRD Tata Sports Complex and the Tata Football Academy (TFA).

Education

XLRI, one of India's oldest and most prestigious business schools.

Street Food Legends

Jamshedpur's street food is legendary. From the posh lanes of Bistupur to the bustling markets of Sakchi, the flavors are unforgettable.

Pani Puri

Also known as Golgappa. The tamarind water here has a unique blend of spices. The stalls near Jubilee Park gate are a must-visit.

Masala Cold Drink

A Jamshedpur specialty! A mix of cola/lemon soda with a secret blend of spices (masala) and lemon. Refreshing!

Dosa

Due to a diverse population, the South Indian food scene is strong. The 'Madrasi Hotel' and street carts in Bistupur serve crisp buttery dosas.

Fakira Chanachur

An iconic spicy mix snack. People take packets of 'Fakira Chanachur' as souvenirs from Jamshedpur to other cities.

Iconic Landmarks

From the shimmering Dimna Lake to the elephants of Dalma, explore the outskirts of the Steel City.

Jubilee Park

Gifted by Tata Steel to the city on its golden jubilee. Spread over 200 acres, it features the Moghul Gardens, fountains, and a zoo. The illumination on March 3rd (Founder's Day) is world-famous.

Dimna Lake

Located at the foothills of Dalma, this artificial lake is the main water source for the city and a stunning picnic spot, especially in winter.

Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary

A forest sanctuary famous for its resident elephants. The view of the Steel City lights from the Dalma hilltop at night is breathtaking.
