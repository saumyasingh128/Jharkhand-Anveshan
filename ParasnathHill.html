Spiritual Significance

The Holiest Jain Pilgrimage

Parasnath Hill, also known as Shikharji, is the most important Jain pilgrimage site. It is believed that 20 out of the 24 Jain Tirthankaras (spiritual teachers) attained Moksha (salvation) here, including Lord Parshvanath, the 23rd Tirthankara.

The name "Parasnath" is derived from Lord Parshvanath, while "Shikharji" means "peak of excellence" or "venerable peak." At 1,365 meters (4,478 feet), it is the highest mountain in the state of Jharkhand.

The pilgrimage to Shikharji involves visiting 24 temples (tonks) along the 27-kilometer circular route, each representing one of the Tirthankaras. This sacred journey is considered one of the most significant acts in Jainism.
