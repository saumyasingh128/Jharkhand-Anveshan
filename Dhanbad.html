THE BLACK DIAMOND CITY

DHANBAD

Fueling the nation's energy. Home to the prestigious IIT (ISM), scenic dams, and a spirit of resilience.

Explore City Watch Video

ENERGY HUB

Dhanbad is one of the fastest-growing cities in India. It is famous for its rich coalfields, but it is also a center for education and scenic beauty.

More Than Just Coal

While Jharia's coal mines power the nation, Dhanbad takes pride in IIT (ISM) Dhanbad, an institute of national importance founded in 1926 with British architecture.

The city is undergoing a rapid transformation with new flyovers, malls, and parks, balancing its industrial heritage with modern living.

Education

IIT (ISM) is the crown jewel of technical education.

Economy

BCCL, Tata Steel, and ECL headquarters drive the economy.

ICONIC SPOTS

Escape the city dust and explore the serene waters and spiritual centers of Dhanbad.

Maithon Dam

"Mother's Abode". Located on the Barakar River, this massive dam offers boating, deer parks, and the famous Kalyaneshwari Temple nearby. It's the biggest reservoir of DVC.

Shakti Mandir

Situated in the heart of the city, this temple dedicated to Goddess Durga draws thousands of devotees. The 'Akhand Jyoti' (eternal flame) is brought from Jwalaji.

Bhatinda Falls

A cascading waterfall surrounded by rocky terrains near Moonidih. It is a perfect picnic spot for nature lovers who enjoy raw landscapes.

Street Fuel

From spicy Litti Chokha to sweet Rabri, Dhanbad's food scene is as robust as its industry.

Litti Chokha

The staple food. Roasted wheat balls stuffed with sattu, dipped in ghee, and served with spicy mashed eggplant (chokha). Available at every chowk.

Gupchup (Chaat)

Dhanbad's Gupchup (Panipuri) is famously spicy. The chaat stalls near Bank More and Hirapur serve tangy mixtures that wake up your senses.

Rabri & Sweets

After a spicy meal, locals love the thick, creamy Rabri. The sweet shops in Jharia and Dhanbad main market are legendary.
